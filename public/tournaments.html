Tournaments & cups

A tournament usually means several national teams (or first-class sides) meeting in a round-robin or group stage, then knockouts. These events sit alongside regular bilateral series (two countries touring each other) and are often used to crown a regional champion or prepare teams for ICC events.

Asia Cup

Contested by Asian Full Members (and sometimes Associates). It has rotated between ODI and T20 editions depending on the cycle leading into global events. It is a high-intensity derby-rich competition—India, Pakistan, Sri Lanka, Bangladesh, Afghanistan, and others.

ICC Champions Trophy (historical)

Often described as a “mini World Cup,” it featured top-ranked ODI sides in a shorter field. The ICC has adjusted its men’s limited-overs portfolio over time (World Cup formats, Super League windows), so treat the Champions Trophy as an important part of history even if future editions depend on ICC scheduling decisions.

Regional championships

  • Africa — pathways for Associates and development sides toward global qualifiers.
  • Europe & Americas — qualifier tournaments feed into ICC global events.
  • Pacific / Oceania — emerging nations compete for spots in World Cup qualifiers.

These tournaments affect rankings and qualification—not just pride—because ICC World Cups often use qualifying leagues and playoffs years in advance.

Bilateral series vs tournaments

Bilateral: Country A tours Country B for Tests/ODIs/T20Is—great for rivalry narratives (e.g. Ashes). Tournament: Many teams in one venue cluster or shared hosting—great for unpredictability and net-run-rate drama. Both are essential to the calendar.
