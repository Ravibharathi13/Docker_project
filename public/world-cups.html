ICC World Cups

The International Cricket Council runs the flagship global tournaments. The men’s Cricket World Cup (50-over) and men’s T20 World Cup are the best known; the women’s programme has grown with its own ODI and T20 World Cups. Host nations and formats evolve—this page explains the roles of each event at a high level.

Men’s Cricket World Cup (ODI)

Traditionally the sport’s biggest single trophy in the 50-over format. Teams qualify via rankings and global qualifiers. The tournament blends group stages and knockouts (exact structure varies by edition). Winning it is often seen as the pinnacle of one-day international cricket.

Men’s T20 World Cup

First held in 2007, it matches the explosive T20 format with national pride. Upsets are common—Associate nations have produced famous wins. Host rotation brings the carnival to different continents and encourages growth markets.

Women’s World Cups

The women’s ODI World Cup has a long lineage; the women’s T20 World Cup delivers compact, high-drama matches. Professional leagues and stronger pathways have deepened squads globally. Prize money, broadcast coverage, and scheduling parity remain active topics across the sport.

Qualification pathway

ICC uses rankings, league cycles (where applicable), and regional qualifiers so Associate countries can dream of reaching the main draw. That pathway is separate from franchise leagues—you might see the same players in IPL or The Hundred and later representing their country in a World Cup qualifier.

World Cup vs leagues

World Cup

National teams, ICC trophies, qualification drama, multi-week spotlight for the whole country.

League

Franchises, city identities, entertainment product across a season; players wear league kits, not national whites or coloured country uniforms.
