Twenty20 (T20)

Each innings lasts twenty overs (120 legal deliveries). Matches often finish in about three hours—compact enough for evening entertainment and television windows. The same laws of cricket apply, but fielding restrictions and tactical aggression differ sharply from Tests.

T20 International (T20I)

National teams wear official kits and earn ICC rankings. Bilateral series and the T20 World Cup define prestige. Upsets happen because a single over can change momentum; Associate nations have memorable wins against Full Members.

Franchise T20 leagues

Domestic leagues (see Leagues) borrow the T20 rule set but use franchises and drafts. Players may represent different leagues in different countries across the year while still honouring central contracts with their national boards.

Indian Premier League (IPL)

The IPL is India’s flagship men’s T20 franchise league, organised by the BCCI. City-based franchises (the number of teams has varied by season) play a round-robin–style league stage followed by playoffs. Squads are built through player auctions and retention rules; overseas stars join Indian internationals and emerging domestic talent. It is one of the most watched T20 leagues worldwide and usually sits in the global calendar between international windows and other franchise competitions.

At a glance

  • Format — T20 (20 overs per innings); IPL-specific playing conditions apply on top of the Laws of Cricket.
  • Season — Typically held across March–May in India (occasionally with neutral or overseas venues if scheduling requires).
  • Broadcast & fans — High attendance and prime-time matches; exact timings shift each year.

Typical season schedule (phases)

Exact dates, venues, and the number of league matches change every season. Use the table below as a general template; for the current year’s fixtures, always check the official IPL site.

Phase What usually happens Rough place in the window
League stage Each franchise plays a fixed set of matches (groups or tables may vary by edition); points and net run rate decide standings. Early–mid season (bulk of March–April)
Playoffs Top four qualify: Qualifier 1, Eliminator, Qualifier 2 (order per IPL playoff rules for that year). Late season (often May)

Tactics in brief

  • Powerplay — only two fielders outside the inner circle (standard laws); boundaries matter.
  • Middle overs — wrist-spin and variations to squeeze scoring.
  • Death overs — yorkers, slower balls, fearless hitting.

Specialists emerge: finishers who thrive from ball one, mystery spinners, multi-phase bowlers who change pace each delivery.

T20 vs ODI vs Test

T20 rewards risk–reward per ball; ODI asks for gears across fifty overs; Test asks for discipline across days. Many players succeed in two formats; mastering all three is the hallmark of great modern careers.
